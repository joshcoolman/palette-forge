# Naming

A palette's name is the first thing a person feels about it, before they read a
single hex. The deterministic namer already gives every palette a solid two-word
name (`Harbor Hush`, `Jewel Velvet`) in the voice of the curated seed swatches.
Your job is to do *better* than that when asked — a name with more culture in it,
more surprise, more of a point of view — while staying in the same shape.

## The shape

**Exactly two words.** Each capitalized. No punctuation, no numbers, no trailing
period. It has to sit in a small label next to the swatches, so it stays short.

`Tuscan Dusk` · `Cobalt Riot` · `Sumi Ink` · `Brutalist Bloom` · `Velvet Heresy`

## The voice

Read the colors first — the ground is the hero, the accent is the surprise — and
name the *mood the pair speaks*, not the hues. Reach past the color word.

- **Materials and places** carry more than adjectives: `Foundry`, `Linen`,
  `Harbor`, `Kiln`, `Marble`, `Tannery`.
- **Cultural and film references** are welcome when they fit — a era, a movement,
  a texture of a place. Wear it lightly; never force it.
- **A little wit** beats earnest mood-board words. `Quiet Riot` over `Calm Energy`.

## What to avoid

- Don't restate the color theory (`Complementary Blue`, `Triadic Set`).
- Don't pile on two adjectives (`Warm Soft`) — at least one word should be a noun
  with an image in it.
- Don't be generic (`Modern Palette`, `Color Story`, `Nice Mix`).
- Keep it clean and inclusive — nothing a stranger would wince at.

When in doubt, pick the name that makes someone want to use the palette.
