# Harmony

The palette types a direction can be taken toward, and when each fits.

- **Monochrome** — one hue, varied in lightness and saturation. Calm, focused, editorial. The accent is the same hue pushed to its most saturated. Risk: can feel flat — earn interest through value range.
- **Analogous** — neighboring hues (within ~30–40° on the wheel). Warm, cohesive, organic. Pick one hue to lead and let the others support. A good default for image-derived palettes.
- **Complementary** — two opposite hues. Punchy, high-energy, clear figure/ground. Use one as the neutral cast and the opposite as the accent; don't let both fight at full saturation.
- **Triadic** — three evenly spaced hues. Playful, balanced, brand-forward. Hard to keep calm — desaturate two and let one lead.
- **Editorial** — high-contrast, near-neutral grounds with a single saturated accent. Confident, magazine-like. The neutrals carry a faint temperature; the accent is the only loud color.

Temperature and mood: warm casts (reds, oranges, yellows) feel inviting and energetic; cool casts (blues, greens) feel calm and trustworthy. Choose a temperature deliberately and keep the neutral family consistent with it.
