## Radically-minimal color-theorist (`ca22dbb`)

Replaced the 109-line `color-theorist.md` with five sentences (+ the verbatim JSON
contract): a mission, multi-hue permission, a plain darkness/white guardrail. Tuned live
against a Minecraft brief (saved to `eval/prompts.md`) on sonnet, using the dev eval bar
and headless runner, reading hue/lightness/saturation back from `eval/runs.jsonl`.

The arc, observed in the data:
- **Baseline (verbose prompt):** every palette one hue + a same-family accent (neutral hue
  spans 3–24°); dark grounds near-black + over-saturated (L 7–11%, S 41–62%).
- **Minimal + multi-hue permission:** genuine cross-hue palettes where the brief invites it
  (green ground + gold secondary + red, 76–135° apart) on the playful Minecraft brief; a
  serious/industrial brief stayed disciplined (1 wild card, restrained darks) — **the prompt
  self-calibrates boldness to the brief, untold.**
- **"Surprise us — one or two of the six go bold":** the model scattered the wild cards
  (positions 1, 2, 5, not just the front) and even rewrote its own friendly message to "a
  couple of wild cards."

**Why (the thesis):** the verbose "definition of good" was hard to *learn from* — with forty
rules you can't tell which one carries the result, and contradictions hide across sections
(the dark-ground "split brain": "saturated deep ground" in one section, "no pure black" in
another, never reconciled). A five-sentence prompt is legible: a stranger feels safe editing
it, which is what makes it maintainable. Goal restated by the user: easy on us, easy on
everyone else, easy for the model.

**Learned (a real eval lesson):** vague guardrails don't bind. "We don't want darks tending
toward 100% black" left grounds at L 4–10% — the model reads near-black as "not pure black,"
so compliant. A concrete-ish floor (a lightness range) is the lever that would actually bite;
left as a known next step since the user judged the current darkness expressive and good
enough ("not curing cancer here").

**Mechanism unchanged:** `knowledge/color-theorist.md` IS the system prompt, sent verbatim
via `generationSystemPrompt()`. The markdown file is the knob; tuning = editing prose, no
code. Gate green (`tsc`, `eslint`, 73/73, `build`).

## Dev eval bar: role × brief; Interior Designer persona (`41289f2`)

Turned the manual `GENERATION_KNOWLEDGE` swap (verbose↔minimal, done by hand all afternoon)
into a **Role dropdown** beside the brief dropdown — the eval bar is now a role × brief
matrix. New `knowledge/roles/` folder holds dev-only alternate personas;
`roles/interior-designer.md` is the worked example (the minimal color-theorist shape, a warm
Pottery-Barn persona). `knowledge-loader.ts` globs `roles/*.md`, merges them into the
basename map, and exports `ROLE_FILES`; a **DEV-gated override** in `prompt-palettes.ts`
(`setGenerationRoleOverride` / `currentGenerationRole`) makes `generationSystemPrompt()`
honor the pick. Each run now stamps its `role` into `runs.jsonl` — self-describing, closes
the attribution gap. Verified live: interior-designer × wellness-yoga returned the warm/
livable sensibility ("serene and restorative", "gentle and nourishing"), and the log carried
`"role": "interior-designer.md"`.

**Why:** "role" never enters the shipped `Source`/journey model — it's purely a dev lever;
the picker is `DEV`-only and tree-shaken, so the live app always uses `color-theorist.md`.
This makes the "copy a role, make your own" pattern concrete: drop a `.md` in
`knowledge/roles/`, it appears in the picker automatically — one file per persona, one thin
call site, no framework. Gate green (`tsc`, `eslint`, 73/73, `build`).
